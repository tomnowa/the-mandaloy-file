# The Mondaloy File

An open-source investigation dashboard documenting the disappearances of personnel connected to Mondaloy, a strategically critical nickel-based superalloy used in US national security rocket propulsion.

## What this is

A single-page static site synthesising publicly available information from patent records, SEC filings, law enforcement press releases, news reporting, and open-source investigative work. It does not assert that any disappearance is the result of foul play. It presents documented facts, flags claims and inferences, and identifies specific questions that remain unanswered.

## Sections

1. **What is Mondaloy** — The alloy, the engineering problem it solved, and why it matters for US national security space launch
2. **The Three People** — The inventor, the qualifier, and the funder who held the complete institutional memory of Mondaloy production
3. **Timeline** — Chronology from invention through the current disappearance cluster, including corporate transfers
4. **The Cluster** — Five senior cleared personnel who vanished without a trace within a 10-month window
5. **Statistics** — Poisson base-rate test comparing observed vs expected disappearances
6. **Corporate Chain** — Seven ownership transfers in thirty years, and the CFIUS gap in current SEC filings
7. **Deletion Pattern** — Four items discussing the Reza memorial or phone evidence removed across four platforms
8. **Unanswered Questions** — Eight specific, checkable questions resolvable with public records or subpoena power

## Evidence standard

Every claim is tagged with one of four labels:

- **Documented** — On the public record
- **Claimed** — Stated by a named source, not independently verified
- **Inferred** — Analytical conclusion from documented facts
- **Disputed** — Actively contested between credible sources
